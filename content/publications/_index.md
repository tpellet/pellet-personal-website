## Academic Publications

### Rigid Production Networks
**Journal of Monetary Economics** | Volume 137, pp. 86-102 | July 2023
*Thomas Pellet, Alireza Tahbaz-Salehi*

Studies how informational frictions and quantity rigidities in production networks affect aggregate output and inflation. Shows that incomplete information dampens the impact of productivity and demand shocks, while making positive demand shocks more inflationary.

[ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S030439322300051X) | [Kellogg](https://www.kellogg.northwestern.edu/faculty/research/detail/2023/rigid-production-networks/)

### A Year of Rising Dangerously? The U.S. Stock Market Performance in the Aftermath of the Presidential Election
**Journal of Policy Modeling** | Volume 40(3), pp. 489-502 | 2018
*Olivier Blanchard, Christopher G. Collins, Mohammad R. Jahan-Parvar, Thomas Pellet, Beth Anne Wilson*

Examines the 25% S&P 500 increase from the 2016 election to end of 2017. Concludes more than half can be attributed to higher actual and expected dividends, with general economic improvement and decreased policy uncertainty as main factors.

[ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0161893818300528)

## Policy Briefs

### Sector Gains Are Uneven under 2017 Tax Law
**PIIE Policy Brief 18-18** | September 2018
*Thomas Pellet*

Analysis showing the 2017 tax law affects corporate profits unevenly across sectors. Sectors with previously high effective tax rates benefit most with stock price increases of close to 10%.

[Read at PIIE](https://www.piie.com/publications/policy-briefs/sector-gains-are-uneven-under-2017-tax-law)
